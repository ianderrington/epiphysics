## The Structural Vulnerability

If $X$ is a representation ([Part 0]../theory/00_prelude.md)) and fields are aggregates of representations ([Part 1, Section 6]../theory/01_generalized_mechanics.md)), then a fundamental vulnerability follows: **deliberately injecting false representations distorts the fields that other entities navigate by.**

This is not a political observation. It is a structural consequence of any system where entities couple to representational fields rather than to reality directly ([Hoffman's Interface Theory](https://doi.org/10.1093/oso/9780393254693.001.0001)).

## Propaganda as Field Manipulation

A reputation field $\Phi_{\text{rep}}^{(D)}(E) = \sum_j w_j \cdot r_j^{(D)}(E)$ is the aggregate of observers' representations. If an agent systematically injects false representations — mischaracterizing an entity's state, fabricating evidence, amplifying distorted signals — the field shifts. Other entities, navigating by the field gradient ($F^i = T^i{}_j \nabla^j \Phi$), are pushed toward attractors that serve the manipulator, not toward states that reflect reality.

This is propaganda formalized: the deliberate distortion of the representational field to redirect other entities' trajectories. It works because entities couple to the field, not to reality directly. A sufficiently distorted field can make entities act against their own persistence — consuming resources that harm them, supporting structures that exploit them, attacking allies and defending threats.

## Information Pollution as Entropy Injection

[Recursive AI training on AI-generated content](../../the_future/information_pollution_and_recursive_training.md) is a specific case: each generation of AI output slightly degrades the representational fidelity, accumulating errors that compound across training cycles. In Epimechanics' terms, this is **entropy injection into the representational substrate** — each cycle increases $\dot{S}_{\text{int}}$ of the information ecosystem without a corresponding increase in $\dot{R}_{\text{repair}}$. The representations degrade. The fields they generate become noisier. Entities navigating by those fields make worse decisions.

[Disinformation campaigns](../../the_future/governance/shadow_disinformation.md) operate by the same mechanism at the social level: flooding the representational environment with false or misleading $X$'s increases the effective $\mathcal{T}_{\text{env}}$ (environmental noise temperature). This lowers the Carnot ceiling ([efficiency limits](./applications/efficiency_limits.md)) for every entity operating in that environment — collective capacity for directed action drops, even if individual entities are unaffected, because the shared field they navigate by has been degraded.

## Why This Matters for Survival

Epimechanics defines soul as $\mathbf{R}(E,t)$ — the representational footprint an entity leaves in others' trajectories. Entities with positive soul-sign (relative to a value basis $\mathbf{v}$ oriented toward long-term flourishing) expand others' capacity for directed action. Entities with negative soul-sign contract it.

Representational manipulation is anti-soul in the framework's precise sense: it degrades the representational fields that enable collective navigation, reducing aggregate free energy $\mathcal{F}$ and increasing environmental noise $\mathcal{T}_{\text{env}}$. At sufficient scale, it threatens the persistence of the representational infrastructure itself — the shared fields (cultural norms, institutional trust, epistemic standards) that enable entities to coordinate rather than collide.

The framework predicts: **systems that do not maintain representational fidelity — that allow unchecked entropy injection into their information substrates — will see their effective Carnot ceiling drop toward zero.** No amount of internal optimization can compensate for a degraded field.

The only interventions that work are:
1. **Reducing $\dot{S}_{\text{info}}$** (source control — stopping the injection)
2. **Increasing $\dot{R}_{\text{repair}}$** (epistemic infrastructure — fact-checking, source verification, institutional trust maintenance)
3. **Reducing coupling to the degraded field** (epistemic insulation — but this trades coordination capacity for noise immunity, at a cost to collective action)

This is a structural prediction: representational systems have a maintenance cost, and neglecting that maintenance is thermodynamically equivalent to letting entropy accumulate in a physical structure. The structure degrades. Eventually it fails.

### Testable Predictions

> **Testable prediction (P-InfoAttack-1).** Attacks on an institution's informational repair substrate (corrupting training materials, distorting cultural norms, degrading documentation) produce longer recovery times than attacks on components (firing people, destroying equipment) of equivalent resource cost. Testable by comparing recovery timescales from resource destruction vs informational corruption in matched organizational contexts.

> **Testable prediction (P-InfoAttack-2).** The energy cost to destabilize an actively-maintained entity through informational attack (corrupting repair substrate) is orders of magnitude lower than through direct structural attack (overwhelming the basin). Testable: compare resource expenditure for successful destabilization via direct vs informational channels across historical cases.
