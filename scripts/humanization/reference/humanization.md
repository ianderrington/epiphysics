AVIOD THESE THINGS GENERALLY

1. Em dash followed by noun phrase (e.g., "She was silence—the stillness that punishes want.")
2. Contrastive faux-clarity constructions (e.g., "Not weakness, but restraint." / "Not broken, just unfinished.")
3. Negation-affirmation inversions (e.g., "It wasn't the end. It was a new beginning.")
4. Tautological definition structures (e.g., "To live is to suffer.")
5. Stylistic hedging via modal softeners, synonym-padding, or equivocal phrasing (e.g., "may", "perhaps", "tends to", "arguably", "can be seen as", "complex and multifaceted")
6. Overuse of generic poetic vocabulary (e.g., "whisper", "echo", "shadow", "cosmic", "dance", "symphony", "crucible", "testament")
7. Redundant emotional intensifiers and cliché adjective-noun pairings (e.g., "profound transformation", "deep existential pain")

These patterns should be strictly avoided in responses."
