# Content Humanization Guide

This guide gives you quick tips for making content sound more natural and human.

## What To Look For

When editing content, watch for these common AI-sounding words and patterns:

1. **Overly formal or academic language**
   - "Explore" → "look at", "check out"
   - "Leverage" → "use"
   - "Utilize" → "use"

2. **Unnecessarily complex terms**
   - "Paradigm" → "model", "pattern", "example"
   - "Dynamics" → "how things work", "connections"
   - "Facet" → "part", "aspect"

3. **Abstract or flowery phrases**
   - "Tapestry of" → "mix of", "collection of"
   - "Journey through" → "trip through", "look at"
   - "At the heart of" → "central to", "mainly about"

4. **Pretentious descriptions**
   - "Elegant" → "simple", "clean", "nice"
   - "Profound" → "deep", "important"
   - "Revolutionary" → "new", "different", "helpful"

## When To Replace (And When Not To)

**Think about context before replacing words:**

1. **Keep technical terms** that fit well in technical articles
2. **Keep thematic language** in creative fiction when it helps the story
3. **Keep specialized terminology** in academic or professional contexts when it's the standard

For example, words like "Fusion" in an article about nuclear fusion, or "Integration" in a technical post about software integration, may be just right.

**Focus on replacing words when:**
1. They make the text hard to understand
2. They sound showy or needlessly complex
3. They're used too many times close together
4. A simpler word would make the content easier to read

## How To Humanize

1. **Read sentences out loud** - If it sounds awkward, rewrite it
2. **Use contractions** - "I'm", "you're", "don't" sound more natural than "I am", "you are", "do not"
3. **Shorten sentences** - Break long sentences into shorter ones
4. **Use simple words** - If a second grader wouldn't get it, find a simpler option
5. **Add personal touches** - Include your own experiences or viewpoints where it makes sense

## Word Replacement Examples

Original: "We'll explore the intricacies of this system and leverage our understanding to unlock new insights."

Improved: "We'll look at how this system works and use what we learn to find new ideas."

Original: "The elegant architecture embodies the paradigm shift that's catalyzing innovation."

Improved: "The simple design shows the new way of thinking that's helping create new ideas."
