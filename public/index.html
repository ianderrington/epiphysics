Epiphysics

The empirical science of Epimechanics

All representations have mechanical structure. That's just calculus — apply F = dp/dt to any time-varying quantity.

Good representations have simple mechanical structure — simple enough to predict dynamics at minimal computational cost.

The existence of an optimal representation is a theorem (Shannon, Rissanen, Solomonoff). That it has Lagrangian structure is the conjecture.

X* = argmin C(X, ε)

The optimal representation minimizes predictive cost.

Key Results

  • PROVEN Quadratic kinetic energy is minimum-cost among real-analytic, time-reversible systems
  • PROVEN Symmetries reduce effective prediction dimension (Noether)
  • PROVEN Sparse coupling enables parallel prediction
  • ARGUED Lagrangian framework compresses better than generic ODE for symmetric systems
  • OPEN Does this hold for irreversible systems?
  • OPEN Can the Lagrangian be derived from bond network structure?
